Most file tools assume one thing: that organizing means breaking files apart.

FileFlow doesn't.

It understands the difference between a messy inbox and a carefully structured archive — and treats them differently.

How FileFlow works

1. Not everything should be "organized" the same way

Some folders are dumps. Downloads. WeChat. Desktop. They're meant to be cleared.

Others are archives. Projects. Deliverables. History. They're meant to stay intact.

FileFlow is designed around this distinction.

2. AI suggests. You stay in control.

FileFlow uses rules and AI to suggest where files belong. It never acts without your confirmation.

Every action follows the same principle:

Plan → Commit → Undo

You can review everything. You can undo anything.

3. Order, maintained — not enforced

You don't "organize everything once".

You spend a few minutes each week, reviewing suggestions. FileFlow handles the rest — safely.

What FileFlow is (and isn't)

FileFlow is:

  • Local-first
  • Fully undoable
  • Designed for long-term retrievability

FileFlow is not:

  • A cloud storage service
  • An auto-executing AI
  • A system that breaks your existing structure

Trust & Privacy

  • Files never leave your machine
  • AI providers are optional and user-controlled (BYOK)
  • No tracking. No telemetry. No hidden sync
  • iCloud is treated as a location, not a service

Your file system remains yours.
